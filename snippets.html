Card 01
Vestigial Sideband (VSB)

Even though SSB-SC modulation reduces the bandwidth requirement for transmission, SSB-SC signals are relatively difficult to generate due to the difficulty in isolating the desired sideband.

In VSB, a compromise between SSB and DSB-SC, one sideband is passed almost completely whereas just a vestige of the other sideband is retained.

The transmitted vestige compensates for the amount removed from the desired sideband.

Transmission bandwidth = \(\mathrm{f_m + f_v}\) where \(\mathrm{f_v}\) is the width of the vestigial sideband.

Generation of VSB
🔍 Expand
Block Diagram — VSB Generator

\(\mathrm{v(t)=A_c m(t)\cos 2\pi f_c t}\)

\(\mathrm{s(t)=v(t)\otimes h(t)}\)

Time domain VSB signal:

\(\mathrm{\dfrac{A_c}{2}[m(t)\cos 2\pi f_c t - m_s(t)\sin 2\pi f_c t]}\)

Frequency domain:

\(\mathrm{S(f)=\dfrac{A_c}{2}[M(f-f_c)+M(f+f_c)]\cdot H(f)}\)

Detection of VSB
🔍 Expand
Block Diagram — VSB Demodulator

Output of product modulator: \(\mathrm{A_c s(t)\cos 2\pi f_c t}\). Applying F.T.:

\(\mathrm{V(f)=\dfrac{A_c}{2}[S(f-f_c)+S(f+f_c)]}\)

\(\mathrm{[M(f-2f_c)+M(f)]H(f-f_c)+[M(f+2f_c)+M(f)]H(f+f_c)}\)

Output of LPF:

\(\mathrm{V_0(f)=\dfrac{A_c^2}{4}M(f)[H(f-f_c)+H(f+f_c)]}\)
